Why Tanzania?

Tanzania is the heart of African safari. The Serengeti's endless plains host the world's largest concentration of large mammals, while the Ngorongoro Crater harbors one of Africa's highest densities of predators. Add the snow-capped peaks of Mount Kilimanjaro and the pristine beaches of Zanzibar, and Tanzania offers an unrivaled diversity of experiences.

Whether you're planning your first game drive or your fifth, Tanzania's national parks and conservation areas deliver encounters with lion, leopard, elephant, buffalo, and rhino—the celebrated Big Five—that few destinations can match.

Top Safari Destinations in Tanzania

Serengeti National Park

Witness the Great Migration, where over a million wildebeest traverse endless golden plains in search of fresh grazing.

Ngorongoro Conservation Area

A volcanic caldera teeming with wildlife, offering some of Africa's best game viewing in a contained, dramatic setting.

Tarangire National Park

Elephant highways and ancient baobab trees create an atmospheric counterpoint to the Serengeti's famous plains.

Zanzibar

Spice islands off Tanzania's coast, offering pristine beaches, Stone Town heritage, and world-class diving.

The Great Migration

The annual wildebeest migration between Tanzania's Serengeti and Kenya's Maasai Mara is one of nature's greatest spectacles. Over 1.5 million wildebeest, accompanied by hundreds of thousands of zebra and gazelle, follow ancient migration patterns in search of rain-ripened grass.

  • December-March: Calving season in the southern Serengeti, where predators gather for easy prey
  • April-June: The dramatic river crossings begin as the herds move north
  • July-October: The herds reach the Maasai Mara, with river crossings at crocodile-infested waters

When to Visit Tanzania

Our seasonal guide details the optimal timing for your safari:

  • Dry Season (June-October): Peak safari season. Animals concentrate around water sources, making game viewing exceptional. The Great Migration river crossings occur during this period.
  • Green Season (November-May): Luscious landscapes, newborn animals, fewer crowds, and lower prices. Short rains bring dramatic skies and excellent bird watching.

Types of Safaris

  • Classic Game Drives: The quintessential safari experience in modified 4x4 vehicles with open roofs
  • Walking Safaris: Experience the bush on foot with armed guides for an intimate, grounded perspective
  • Hot Air Balloon: Float silently over the Serengeti at sunrise for a unique aerial perspective of wildlife
  • Fly-in Safaris: Maximize time with inter-park flights, ideal for shorter trips

Practical Information

  • Visa: Most nationalities receive a visa on arrival (approximately $50-100 for single entry)
  • Health: Yellow fever vaccination required if arriving from endemic areas; malaria prophylaxis recommended
  • Currency: Tanzanian Shilling (TSH); US dollars widely accepted at lodges and for park fees
  • Best Parks: Serengeti, Ngorongoro, Tarangire, and Ruaha offer exceptional wildlife experiences
